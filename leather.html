Leather export

Patent Leather Patent leather is a glossy and highly reflective leather that has been coated with a varnish or lacquer finish.
Exotic leather refers to leather made from the hides of non-traditional animals such as alligators, crocodiles, snakes, ostriches, or stingrays
Corrected-grain leather is created by applying an artificial grain texture to the surface of split leather.

Suede leather is created by sanding or buffing the underside of split leather, resulting in a soft and fuzzy surface.
Split leather is derived from the lower layers of the hide after the top grain is separated.

Nubuck leather is top grain leather that has been buffed or sanded on the grain side to create a velvety texture.
